# PSV Interactive Simulator

> ⚠️ Experimental educational simulator. Not validated. Not intended for clinical use.

A browser-based simulator of Pressure Support Ventilation (PSV) developed for teaching respiratory physiology and patient–ventilator interaction.

The simulator runs entirely client-side using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript and requires no installation or server-side infrastructure.

## Educational Objectives

The simulator is intended to facilitate understanding of:

- Pressure Support Ventilation (PSV)
- Patient–ventilator interaction
- Triggering and cycling mechanisms
- Respiratory effort and respiratory drive
- Effects of compliance and resistance
- Auto-PEEP and dynamic hyperinflation
- Ventilator and patient leaks
- Common patient–ventilator asynchronies
- Bedside occlusion maneuvers

## Features

### Ventilator Controls

- PEEP
- Pressure Support
- Flow Trigger
- Expiratory Trigger Sensitivity (ETS)
- Rise Time
- Circuit Leak
- Backup Ventilation

### Patient Model

- Respiratory System Compliance (CRS)
- Respiratory System Resistance (Rrs)
- Functional Residual Capacity (FRC)
- Auto-PEEP
- Patient Leak

### Respiratory Drive

- Neural Respiratory Rate
- Inspiratory Effort Magnitude
- Neural Inspiratory Time
- Multiple Effort Waveforms

### Real-Time Waveforms

- Airway Pressure (Paw)
- Flow
- Volume
- Esophageal Pressure (Pes)
- Pmus-equivalent signal
- Ventilator State

### Preset Scenarios

- Normal PSV
- Overassistance
- Underassistance
- Ineffective Efforts
- Premature Cycling
- Delayed Cycling
- Auto-triggering due to Leaks

### Simulated Occlusion Maneuvers

- P0.1
- ΔPocc
- MIF / NIF
- PMI

### Data Export

- CSV export of generated waveforms

## Disclaimer

### Educational Use Only

This software is an experimental educational tool intended exclusively for teaching and learning purposes.

### Simplified Physiological Model

The physiological and ventilator models implemented in this simulator are simplified representations of complex biological and mechanical systems.

The simulator does not reproduce the behavior of any specific commercial ventilator and should not be considered a validated physiological model.

### Not for Clinical Use

This simulator must not be used for:

- Patient care
- Clinical decision-making
- Ventilator setup or optimization
- Ventilator troubleshooting in real patients
- Medical advice
- Research requiring validated physiological simulation

Any values, waveforms, measurements, or indices generated by the simulator should be considered illustrative only.
